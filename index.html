// ABOUT

Learning systems by taking them apart

I'm a cybersecurity Researcher with a deep interest in operating systems and how they actually work underneath the interfaces we take for granted. I spend my time as a Linux enthusiast, exploring the kernel, the filesystem, and the way processes and permissions interact. From there, my curiosity naturally extends into Android and iOS — two very different operating systems built on very different security models, both worth understanding in depth.


I'm particularly drawn to vulnerability research: the discipline of asking "what happens if this assumption is wrong?" and following that question with patience, tools, and method. I'm still early in that journey, learning reverse engineering, basic fuzzing workflows, and the fundamentals that real researchers build on. I care about depth over speed — understanding a system properly matters more to me than moving fast and skipping the internals.
